<p><p>Geosynthetic Clay Liners (GCLs) are engineered materials used in environmental containment applications, such as landfill liners, mining operations, and water containment systems. They consist of a layer of bentonite clay sandwiched between two geotextiles or bonded to a geomembrane. <p><p>Geosynthetic Clay Liners (GCLs) are composite materials used in environmental containment systems. Nonwoven geotextile-based GCLs consist of needle-punched fabrics bonded to bentonite, offering high permeability and strength. Woven geotextile-based GCLs use tightly woven fabrics, providing enhanced durability and puncture resistance. Natural sodium bentonite GCLs rely on the swelling properties of bentonite clay to create an impermeable barrier. Each type caters to specific applications, such as landfill liners, mining, and water containment, balancing performance, cost, and environmental requirements.</p></p>
