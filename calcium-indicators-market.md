<p><ul><li>Chemical Indicators</li><li>Genetically Encoded Indicators</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The calcium indicators market is primarily divided into two types: chemical indicators and genetically encoded indicators. Chemical indicators are small molecules that can bind to calcium ions, producing measurable fluorescence, often used in laboratory settings for real-time monitoring of calcium levels in various biological processes. In contrast, genetically encoded indicators are proteins engineered to respond to calcium fluctuations, typically utilized in live-cell imaging and studies of cellular dynamics. Both types are essential for advancing research in neurobiology, cardiology, and cell signaling.</p></p>
