<p><p>Wear resistance steel is categorized into three main types based on hardness, measured in Brinell hardness (HB). Steel with HB < 400 is typically used for applications requiring good ductility and moderate wear resistance, such as in mining equipment. HB 400-500 offers a balance of hardness and toughness, suitable for parts that endure high wear, like construction machinery. Lastly, HB > 500 provides exceptional wear resistance, making it ideal for abrasive environments, such as in heavy-duty mining and quarrying applications.</p></p>
