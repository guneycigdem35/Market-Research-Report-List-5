<p><p>Multi-Level Cell (MLC) NAND Flash Memory encompasses two primary market types: 2D and 3D NAND. </p><p>2D NAND is traditional and involves layers of memory cells arranged in a planar layout, often leading to scalability limitations and slower performance. Conversely, 3D NAND utilizes vertically stacked cells, significantly enhancing storage density, speed, and endurance while reducing space requirements. The shift to 3D technology allows for larger capacity drives that are more efficient, making it the preferred choice for modern applications and data demands.</p></p>
