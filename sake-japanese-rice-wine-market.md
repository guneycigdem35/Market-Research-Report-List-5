<p><p>Sake, a traditional Japanese rice wine, comes in various types, each defined by its ingredients and brewing methods. Ordinary Sake is typically mass-produced. Junmai is pure rice sake without additives, offering a fuller flavor. Honjozo includes a small amount of distilled alcohol to enhance aroma. Junmai Ginjo is premium sake brewed with polished rice, delivering a refined taste. Ginjo is also premium but can contain additives, focusing on lightness and fragrance. These classifications cater to diverse consumer preferences in the sake market.</p></p>
