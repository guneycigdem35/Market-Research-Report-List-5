<p><p>The Marine Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cell System market is segmented based on hydrogen storage methods: Compressed Gaseous Hydrogen, Cryogenic Liquid Hydrogen, and Hydrides. Compressed Gaseous Hydrogen involves storing hydrogen at high pressures for direct use in fuel cells, emphasizing efficiency and simplicity. Cryogenic Liquid Hydrogen requires maintaining hydrogen at very low temperatures, providing higher energy density. Hydrides involve chemical storage, where hydrogen is absorbed in metal alloys, offering safety and ease of handling but may involve complex management systems.</p></p>
