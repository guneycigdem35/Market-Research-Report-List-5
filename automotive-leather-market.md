<p><p>The automotive leather market comprises two main types: genuine leather and synthetic leather. Genuine leather, made from animal hides, offers luxury, durability, and a premium feel, making it a preferred choice for high-end vehicles. In contrast, synthetic leather, often made from polyurethane or polyvinyl chloride, provides a cost-effective, versatile, and environmentally friendly alternative. It mimics the look and feel of genuine leather while being easier to maintain and available in various designs and colors, appealing to a broader range of consumers.</p></p>
