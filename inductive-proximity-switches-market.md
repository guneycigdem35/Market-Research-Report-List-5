<p><p>Inductive proximity switches are categorized into DC and AC types based on their power supply. DC-type switches, typically operating at low voltages (like 12V or 24V), provide faster response times and are ideal for applications requiring precise control in automation systems. On the other hand, AC-type switches operate on higher voltage levels (usually 110V or 220V) and can handle larger loads, making them suitable for industrial environments. Both types enhance automation efficiency by detecting metallic objects without direct contact.</p></p>
