<p><p>The HIV drug market consists of various classes designed to combat the virus effectively. Multi-class combination products combine multiple drug classes in a single treatment, enhancing adherence. Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NRTIs) block viral replication by targeting reverse transcriptase. Non-Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NNRTIs) also inhibit this enzyme but act differently. Protease Inhibitors prevent virus maturation. Fusion Inhibitors block virus entry into cells, while Entry Inhibitors specifically target CCR5 co-receptors. Lastly, Integrase Strand Transfer Inhibitors prevent viral DNA integration into the host genome.</p></p>
