<p><ul><li>LLDPE</li><li>LDPE</li><li>HDPE</li><li>PET/BOPET</li><li>PP/BOPP</li><li>CPP</li><li>PVC</li><li>Polyamide/BOPA</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The industrial films market comprises various polymer types, each with distinct properties and applications. LLDPE (Linear Low-Density Polyethylene) offers flexibility and toughness, ideal for packaging. LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene) provides clarity and moisture resistance, used in bags and wraps. HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) is strong and rigid, suitable for heavy-duty packaging. PET/BOPET (Polyethylene Terephthalate/Biaxially Oriented PET) offers high strength and barrier properties, used in food and electronics. PP/BOPP (Polypropylene/Biaxially Oriented PP) is lightweight and heat-resistant, common in labels and packaging. CPP (Cast Polypropylene) is heat-sealable, used in laminates. PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) is durable and versatile, used in construction and healthcare. Polyamide/BOPA (Biaxially Oriented Polyamide) provides excellent barrier properties, ideal for food packaging.</p></p>
