<p><p>The dyestuff market for textile fibers encompasses various types, each suited for different applications. Disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetic fibers, offering vibrant colors. Reactive dyes bond chemically with fibers, ideal for cotton and viscose. Sulfur dyes provide deep shades and excellent wash fastness, often used in denim. Vat dyes are known for their lightfastness and are suitable for cotton. Acid dyes are preferred for protein fibers like wool and silk. Other categories include direct and pigment dyes, catering to diverse textile needs.</p></p>
