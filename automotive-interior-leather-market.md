<p><p>The automotive interior leather market comprises two main types: genuine leather and synthetic leather. Genuine leather, made from animal hides, is valued for its durability, luxurious feel, and breathability, often found in high-end vehicles. Synthetic leather, or faux leather, is engineered from materials like polyurethane or PVC, offering a more affordable, animal-friendly alternative. It mimics the appearance of genuine leather while being easier to maintain and resistant to wear, making it popular in a wide range of vehicles.</p></p>
