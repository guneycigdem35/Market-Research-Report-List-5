<p><p>In the cosmetic market, liquid paraffin is categorized into paraffin-based and cycloalkyl-based types. Paraffin-based liquid paraffin, derived from petroleum, is often used for its moisturizing properties and smooth texture, making it ideal for creams and lotions. Cycloalkyl-based liquid paraffin, on the other hand, has a more refined chemical structure that enhances stability and reduces greasiness, making it suitable for formulations seeking a lighter feel. Both types serve diverse cosmetic applications, benefiting skin hydration and overall product performance.</p></p>
