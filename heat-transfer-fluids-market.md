<p><p>The heat-transfer fluids market comprises three primary types: glycol-based fluids, synthetic fluids, and other fluids. Glycol-based fluids, such as ethylene and propylene glycol, are commonly used for their excellent thermal properties and freeze protection, making them ideal for HVAC systems and industrial processes. Synthetic fluids offer superior thermal stability, high temperature resistance, and low volatility, suitable for specialized applications such as solar thermal and chemical processing. Other fluids include mineral oils and fusible salts, catering to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
