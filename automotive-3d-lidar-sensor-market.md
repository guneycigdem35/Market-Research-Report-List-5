<p><ul><li>Mechanical Lidar</li><li>Solid State Lidar</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Automotive 3D LiDAR Sensor market comprises two main types: Mechanical LiDAR and Solid-State LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR uses rotating components to emit laser beams and capture 3D spatial data, offering high precision but with bulkier designs and higher costs. Solid-State LiDAR, on the other hand, relies on fixed components without moving parts, enabling compact, durable, and cost-effective solutions. Both technologies are pivotal for autonomous vehicles, with Solid-State LiDAR gaining traction due to its scalability and reliability in automotive applications.</p></p>
