<p><p>Electron Capture Detectors (ECD) are sensitive analytical devices used in chromatography for detecting electronegative compounds, such as halogens and functional groups. The market for ECDs is divided into two main types: radioactive and non-radioactive electron capture detectors. Radioactive ECDs utilize a small amount of radioactive material to ionize the carrier gas, enhancing sensitivity. In contrast, non-radioactive ECDs employ alternative ionization methods, offering advantages like safety and regulatory compliance. Both types cater to various applications in environmental monitoring, pharmaceuticals, and chemical analysis.</p></p>
